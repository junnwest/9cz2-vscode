## Friday, June 13, 2026 — WashU Collaborator Meeting

### 1. Membrane restraint approach
Dr. Haddadian mentioned (in a prior meeting with the collaborator) an alternative to the full membrane simulation: **remove the membrane entirely and apply positional restraints to the membrane-embedded TM regions** to simulate membrane rigidity, reducing computational cost significantly. Ask the collaborator:
- Does he still think this is a viable/desirable approach?
- What restraint scheme would be appropriate (flat-bottomed? harmonic? which atoms)?
- How does this compare to explicit membrane in terms of capturing lipid-protein effects relevant to the dome opening question?

### 2. FtsH copy number — 7wi3 (4 FtsH) vs. 9cz2 (2 FtsH)
7wi3 (older, closed structure) has 4 FtsH hexamers; 9cz2 has 2. Dr. Haddadian said 2 is more physiologically correct. Confirm:
- Is the 4-FtsH structure an artifact of C4 symmetry imposition during cryo-EM processing?
- What is the experimental evidence for 2 FtsH in the native assembly?

*Note: Likely answerable — 9cz2 was solved in C1 (no symmetry imposed), 7wi3 was solved with C4 symmetry, which would force 4 FtsH. Ask for direct confirmation.*

### 3. What determines the dome opening location?
Dr. Haddadian said FtsH does not contribute to the asymmetry/opening of the dome. But HflK/C subunits are sequence-identical, so the opening isn't encoded by sequence differences. Then:
- Is the opening position **stochastic** (spontaneous symmetry breaking during assembly)?
- Or is there a deterministic signal — e.g., does FtsH insert first and the ring assembles around it, with the opening forming at a specific geometric position relative to FtsH?
- Does the opening location matter functionally, or can it open anywhere with equivalent effect?

*Note: This may be an open scientific question. Frame it as: "If HflK/C subunits are sequence-identical, is the opening position stochastic or is there a deterministic signal we're missing?"*
